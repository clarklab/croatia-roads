Antenna of the Bura

a short rite for the corridor between the Arena of Pula
and the White City of Kings · one round ≈ fifteen minutes
ⰀⰐⰕⰅⰐⰀ

You are traversing a wire of land strung between two dead empires. Rome ends at Pula. The Kingdom of the Croats waits at Biograd, where kings were crowned and the city was burned for it. The road takes five hours. This rite does not. You play it in short bursts, whenever the mood strikes: tell the Oracle where you are, it deals you seven omens, you hunt them out the window. Fifteen minutes, give or take. Then put the phone down and watch the sea. The Bura — the white wind that falls off the Velebit and capsizes trucks out of principle — will wait. It is very old.

The Eternal Laws

  1. The Law of the Mountain. Inside any tunnel, the word “tunnel” is forbidden. A violator must immediately confess one (1) true secret, aloud, to the mountain. This law is in effect even when the game is not.
  2. The Law of the Witness. An omen counts only if seen with the eyes. Phones showing pictures of omens are mirrors, and mirrors lie.
  3. The Law of the Driver. The driver may witness omens but may never operate the Antenna. The driver is the sacrifice. Treat the driver kindly.
  4. The Law of Roadkill. All roadkill receives four (4) seconds of silence. This is not optional. The Bura counts.
  5. The Law of the Hum. When the Bura Meter fills, all souls aboard must hum a single shared note for ten seconds. The note will choose itself.

Incorporation of Souls

Each traveler presses the bone once and receives a Liburnian travel-name in the old Glagolitic script. Your real name stays in Pula. You retrieve it on the way back, probably.
